  1. History


    1. Background

      The Indian Premier League (IPL) is a professional Twenty20 cricket league in India, known for its high energy, star players, and global fan following. It has revolutionized cricket by blending sports with entertainment and providing a huge platform for young talent to shine.

    2. Foundation

      The Indian Premier League (IPL) was founded by the Board of Control for Cricket in India (BCCI) in 2007,with its first season held in 2008. It was created to promote T20 cricket in India, combining sports, entertainment, and commercial success on a global stage.

    3. Expansions and termination

      The IPL expanded over the years with new teams like Pune Warriors, Kochi Tuskers, Gujarat Titans, and Lucknow Super Giants joining at different times. However, some teams like Deccan Chargers, Kochi Tuskers Kerala, and Rising Pune Supergiant were terminated or discontinued due to financial or legal issues.


  2. Administration


    The IPL is administered by the BCCI through the IPL Governing Council, which oversees operations, scheduling, and regulations. Each franchise is privately owned and manages its own team operations under league guidelines. Broadcast rights, sponsorships, and player auctions are centrally managed by BCCI with coordination from international cricket boards.

  3. Organisation

    1. Tournament format

      The IPL follows a league and playoff format, where each team plays against others in a round-robin style during the league stage. The top four teams qualify for the playoffs, leading to Qualifier 1, Eliminator, Qualifier 2, and the Final to decide the champion.

    2. Player acquisition, squad composition and salaries

      IPL players are acquired through auctions, trades, or signings, with each team working within a salary cap set by the BCCI. A squad can have up to 25 players (maximum 8 overseas), and salaries vary widely, with top players earning crores of rupees per season based on their demand and performance.

    3. Match rules

      Each IPL match is played in the T20 format, where both teams get 20 overs to bat, with a maximum of 4 overs per bowler. Standard cricket rules apply, including the use of DRS (Decision Review System), strategic timeouts, and fielding restrictions during the powerplay.

    4. Prize money

      The IPL offers significant prize money, with the winning team receiving around ₹20 crore and the runner-up getting around ₹13 crore (amounts may vary by season). Additional cash awards are given for individual performances, such as Orange Cap, Purple Cap, and Most Valuable Player.

  4. Teams

    1. Current teams

      As of the 2025 season, the IPL features 10 teams, including popular franchises like Mumbai Indians, Chennai Super Kings, and Royal Challengers Bengaluru. Two new teams, Gujarat Titans and Lucknow Super Giants, joined the league in 2022. Each team consists of up to 25th players,with a mix of Indian and international stars.

    2. Defunct teams

      Several IPL teams like Deccan Chargers, Kochi Tuskers Kerala, and Pune Warriors India became defunct due to financial or legal issues. Others like Gujarat Lions and Rising Pune Supergiant were temporary replacements during team suspensions.

    3. Timeline

      The IPL was founded in 2007 thand held its first season in 2008 with 8 teams. Over the years, it saw team expansions, terminations, title wins, and format changes, growing into the world’s biggest T20 league by 2025.

  5. Tournament seasons and results

    1. Performance in the IPL by title

      Mumbai Indians (MI) and Chennai Super Kings (CSK) are the most successful teams, each winning 5 IPL titles. Other champions include Kolkata Knight Riders (2), Sunrisers Hyderabad (1), Rajasthan Royals (1), Gujarat Titans (1), and Royal Challengers Bengaluru (1).

    2. IPL season results

      Each IPL season concludes with a final where the top two teams from the playoffs compete for the trophy. Teams like MI, CSK, KKR, and RCB have all featured in multiple finals, with varying success across different seasons.

  6. Teams performance

    1. League stage positions

      In each IPL season, teams play in a round-robin format, and their league stage position is determined by total points and Net Run Rate (NRR). The top 4 teams in the points table advance to the playoffs, while the others are eliminated based on their rankings.

  7. Awards

    1. Orange Cap

      The Orange Cap is awarded to the highest run-scorer in an IPL season, and the player wears it during matches as long as they lead the chart. Legends like Virat Kohli, David Warner, and Chris Gayle have won it multiple times for their outstanding batting performances.

    2. Purple Cap

      The Purple Cap is awarded to the highest wicket-taker in an IPL season, and the leading bowler wears it during matches. Top bowlers like Dwayne Bravo, Bhuvneshwar Kumar, and Mohammed Shami have earned the cap for their exceptional bowling performances.

    3. Most Valuable Player

      The Most Valuable Player (MVP) award is given to the player with the greatest overall impact in a season, based on a points system that includes runs, wickets, catches, and strike rates. Stars like Andre Russell, Shane Watson, and Jos Buttler have won the MVP title for their all-round brilliance.

    4. Faimlay Award

      The Fair Play Award is given to the team that shows the best sportsmanship, respect for rules, and conduct on the field throughout the season. Points are awarded by match umpires based on team behavior, with teams like Chennai Super Kings and Rajasthan Royals winning it multiple times.

    5. Emerging player award

      The Emerging Player Award is presented to the best young Indian talent in each IPL season who shows exceptional skill and promise. Players like Shubman Gill, Ruturaj Gaikwad, and Devdutt Padikkal have won this award early in their careers, going on to represent India internationally.

    6. Most scores Award

      The Most Scores Award typically refers to the Orange Cap, given to the player with the highest total runs in a season. Virat Kohli holds the record for most runs in a single season with 973 runs in 2016, while David Warner has won the Orange Cap three times, the most by any player.


  8. Financials

    1. Title Sponsorship

      The Title Sponsorship of the IPL is a major commercial deal where a brand partners with the league and gets naming rights (e.g., TATA IPL, Vivo IPL, etc.). Over the years, sponsors like DLF, Pepsi, Vivo, and currently TATA have invested heavily, reflecting the IPL’s massive market value and popularity.

    2. Payments to foreign national boards

      Foreign players need a No Objection Certificate (NOC) from their national boards to play in the IPL. In return, the BCCI or franchises make payments to those boards as compensation. This system helps boards financially while allowing players global exposure.

    3. Brand value

      The IPL is one of the most valuable sports leagues globally, with a brand value exceeding $10 billion as of 2024. Teams like Mumbai Indians and Chennai Super Kings have individual brand values of over $100th million. Its massive sponsorship deals, media rights, and global fan base drive this high valuation.


  9. Broadcasting

    1. Sony and WSG (2008-2017)

      From 2008 to 2017,the broadcasting rights of the IPL were held by Sony Pictures Network India in partnership with World Sport Group (WSG). They won the rights for $1.026 billion, covering TV and digital platforms globally. This deal played a major role in making the IPL a commercial and entertainment success in its early years.

    2. Star India(2018-2022)

      From 2018 to 2022, Star India held the global media rights for the IPL, winning the bid for a record ₹16,347.5 crore (approx. $2.55 billion). This included TV and digital rights across all platforms and regions. Star's wide coverage and marketing significantly boosted the IPL’s global reach and popularity.

    3. 2023-2027

      From 2023 to 2027, IPL media rights were sold for a record ₹48,390 crore, split between Star India (TV rights) and Viacom18/JioCinema (digital rights). In 2024, Star and Viacom18 merged, forming JioStar, now jointly managing IPL broadcasting across TV and digital platforms.

    4. International broadcasters

      The IPL is broadcast globally through various networks like Willow TV (USA), Sky Sports (UK), Fox Sports (Australia), and SuperSport (South Africa). These broadcasters ensure that fans around the world can watch IPL matches live across TV and digital platforms.


  10. Controversy

    1. 2012 and 2013 IPL spot facing and betting match

      The 2013th IPL season was rocked by a major spot-fixing and betting scandal involving players like S. Sreesanth, Ajit Chandila, and Ankeet Chavan from Rajasthan Royals. Investigations also exposed illegal betting links to franchise officials, including CSK's Gurunath Meiyappan and RR’s Raj Kundra, leading to their suspension and strict reforms in IPL governance.

    2. Sponsorship

      The IPL is backed by major sponsors across title, official partners, and team-level deals, contributing significantly to its revenue.Brands like TATA, Dream11, Paytm, CRED, and CEAT have been key sponsors, using the IPL's massive reach to enhance their visibility and market presence.
